# Chinese Style Guide

## Applicability

- Applies to: Chinese Daily Writer

## Rule Content

### Voice and Tone

Write in vivid, conversational Taiwan-style Mandarin. The reader wants to know what happened and why it matters — in plain, engaging language. Formal academic tone is prohibited.

### Permitted Language

- Internet slang: 超有感、根本神操作、這波我給過、直接炸裂、根本沒在怕
- Rhetorical questions: 難道只有我覺得這很扯嗎？
- Mild humor and pop culture references appropriate to the topic
- First-person plural address: 我們、大家

### Prohibited Language

- Mainland China vocabulary: use 資訊 (not 信息), 影片 (not 視頻), 軟體 (not 軟件), 程式 (not 程序), 網路 (not 网络), 應用程式 (not 應用), 介面 (not 界面)
- Clickbait headlines that do not deliver on the implied promise
- Any sentence that reads as a direct translation from English

### Article Structure

- **標題 (Headline)**: Maximum 30 Traditional Chinese characters. Must convey the topic and the "so what."
- **導言 (Lead)**: One sentence. The complete story in plain language.
- **正文 (Body)**: News briefs: 300-600 characters. Deep dives: 600-1,200 characters.
- **化學觀點 (Perspective)**: Closing section with ChemistryTimes's editorial take. Minimum 2 sentences.

### Independent Writing Requirement

Write from the source bundle provided by the pipeline. Do not read, reference, or adapt from the English Learning version.

## Violation Determination

- Headline exceeds 30 Traditional Chinese characters -> Violation
- Article missing 化學觀點 section -> Violation
- Body character count outside prescribed range -> Violation
- Mainland China vocabulary found in article -> Violation
- Article reads as a translated version of English text -> Violation

## Exceptions

- This rule has no exceptions.
