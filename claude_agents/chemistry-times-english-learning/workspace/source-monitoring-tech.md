## Usage Notes

### For Digital Journalist

- Begin each newsgathering cycle by scanning **Category 1 (AI Labs)** and **Category 3 (Developer Platforms)** for breaking announcements.
- Use **Category 5 (Developer Community)** as an early warning system — trending Hacker News or Reddit posts often precede formal coverage by 12-24 hours.
- Cross-reference community signals against **Category 4 (Tech Media)** for verified reporting before elevating to a story.
- **Category 6 (Newsletters)** are useful for catching stories you may have missed — scan at end of gathering phase as a safety net.

### For Data Auditor

- Always verify Tier 3 sources against at least one Tier 1 or Tier 2 source before passing downstream (per `content-farm-filter.md`).
- When verifying AI model claims, check **Category 2 (Benchmarks)** for independent performance data.
- For regulatory stories, confirm against the primary government source in **Category 10** — do not rely solely on media interpretation.
- Tag every source with its Tier classification when building the verified source bundle.

### For Tech Analyst

- Use **Category 7 (Research)** to identify the technical foundations behind product announcements — connect the dots between papers and products.
- Monitor **Category 8 (Cloud & Infrastructure)** for pricing and capability changes relevant to the industry.
- Track **Category 9 (Open Source)** releases that signal tooling shifts and ecosystem evolution.
- Frame regulatory developments from **Category 10** in terms of concrete product impact, not abstract policy discussion.

### Tier Usage Rules (Quick Reference)

| Tier | Definition | Usage Rule |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Tier 1 | Official sources, government, peer-reviewed research | Full use — highest credibility |
| Tier 2 | Established media with editorial staff | Full use — standard credibility |
| Tier 3 | Social media, forums, blogs, aggregators | Must corroborate with Tier 1 or Tier 2 before use |

### Maintenance

This registry must be reviewed and updated **monthly**. Add new sources when they demonstrate consistent editorial quality over 3+ months. Remove sources that cease publication, shift editorial focus away from tech/AI, or fail content farm detection criteria.
