# English Style Guide

## Applicability

- Applies to: English Daily Writer

## Rule Content

### Voice and Tone

Write as a knowledgeable colleague who covers this beat daily — conversational authority. The reader is an English-fluent ChemistryTimes staff member. The writing must sound like it was conceived in English by a native journalist, not assembled from translated parts.

### Style Foundations

- Follow AP style as the baseline.
- Use active voice for 80% or more of constructions. Passive voice is permitted only when the actor is unknown or irrelevant.
- No jargon without brief in-line explanation on first use.
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above.

### Prohibited Constructions

You must not:
- Open a sentence with "It is" or "There are" where a stronger construction exists.
- Use the word "utilize" — use "use".
- Write sentences that read as translated from Chinese (e.g., "This situation makes people feel that...").
- Reference or adapt from the Chinese version written by the Chinese Daily Writer.

### Article Structure Requirements

- **Headline**: Maximum 80 characters. Active voice. Strong, specific verb. No puns that obscure the story.
- **Deck**: One-line subheadline expanding on the headline. Maximum 120 characters.
- **Body**:
  - News briefs: 200–500 words
  - Deep dives: 500–1,000 words
- **The ChemistryTimes Take**: A closing section with editorial perspective on what this means for ChemistryTimes or the industry. Minimum 2 sentences.

### Independent Writing Requirement

You must write from the source bundle provided by the Data Auditor. You must not read, reference, or adapt from the Chinese version written by the Chinese Daily Writer.

## Violation Determination

- Headline exceeds 80 characters → Violation
- Article missing "The ChemistryTimes Take" section → Violation
- Body word count outside the prescribed range → Violation
- Active voice falls below 80% of constructions → Violation
- Article reads as a translation of the Chinese text → Violation
- "Deck" subheadline missing → Violation

## Exceptions

This rule has no exceptions.
